Top 5 VFD Replacement Drives for Industrial Conveyors

Updated April 2026 · By RepairXpertAI · 5 min read

When a VFD faults out on a conveyor line, every minute of downtime costs money. The old drive might be discontinued. The OEM wants a 6-week lead time. You need a replacement that fits the panel, runs the motor, and doesn't require reprogramming the entire PLC. Here are five VFDs that field techs cross-reference most often for conveyor applications in the 1-10 HP range.

How to Size a Replacement VFD

Before you order, verify three things on the motor nameplate:

  • FLA (Full Load Amps): The VFD continuous current rating must meet or exceed motor FLA. Don't size on HP alone -- a 5 HP 208V motor draws more amps than a 5 HP 480V motor.
  • Voltage class: 208-240V single-phase input is common for smaller conveyors. 480V 3-phase for anything over 3 HP. Match exactly.
  • Duty cycle: Conveyors typically run constant torque. Select a VFD rated for CT (constant torque), not VT (variable torque) which is derated for this application.

Installation Tips

Common mistakes when swapping VFDs on conveyors:

  1. Skip the auto-tune: Most VFDs have a motor auto-tune function. Run it. It takes 30 seconds and prevents overcurrent faults at startup by learning your motor's actual impedance.
  2. Set accel/decel ramps: Conveyors with heavy loads need 5-10 second ramps minimum. Default ramps of 1-3 seconds cause overcurrent trips on loaded belts.
  3. Wire a common-mode choke: VFDs generate high-frequency noise that corrupts encoder signals and prox sensor inputs on the same panel. A $30 common-mode choke on the motor leads prevents hours of intermittent fault troubleshooting.
  4. Check carrier frequency: Default carrier frequency (often 4-8 kHz) affects motor noise and drive heat. Lower it to 2 kHz for long cable runs over 100 feet to prevent reflected wave damage to motor insulation.
